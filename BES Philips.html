Philips Natural Capital: Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services (BES)

The Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services (BES) program of Philips aims to create value for nature and people. The goal is to make our sites greener, more sustainable and more biodiverse. Therefore, we want to increase awareness, knowledge and action on biodiversity and ecosystem services at all Philips’ manufacturing sites. Each site is unique, which leads to a great variety of projects and initiatives to achieve this goal. 

By exploring this map, you will gain insight into Philips manufacturing sites scattered across various regions. Each site represents a microcosm of sustainable practices and environmental stewardship. The map provides a comprehensive overview of the land cover present at each location, ranging from forests and wetlands to streets and gardens. Understanding the diverse land cover is crucial in comprehending the intricate relationship between human activities and the surrounding ecosystems. By visualizing this information, we aim to foster a deeper appreciation for the interconnectedness of nature and manufacturing processes.

In addition to showcasing land cover, this map introduces you to the myriad of initiatives implemented at each Philips site. These initiatives encompass a wide range of activities aimed at preserving and restoring biodiversity and ecosystem services. From reforestation projects and habitat restoration efforts to water conservation and pollination measures, each site has its tailored set of initiatives designed to address local environmental challenges.

One of the key features of this map is the integration of various biodiversity layers, enabling a comprehensive understanding of the ecological significance of each site. These layers include Biodiversity hotspots, highlighting regions of exceptional species richness and endemism; intact forest areas, representing pristine ecosystems with minimal human disturbance; world land cover, showcasing the distribution of different land cover types globally; biodiversity intactness index, measuring the overall health and integrity of ecosystems; and World Protected Areas, displaying the network of protected areas worldwide. By incorporating these biodiversity layers, we aim to highlight the importance of preserving and protecting our natural heritage, and encourage informed decision-making towards sustainable land use and conservation efforts.
